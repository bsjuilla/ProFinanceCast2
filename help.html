Section 01

Getting started.

Do I need to connect a bank account?
No. ProFinanceCast does not integrate with banks, aggregators, or financial institutions of any kind. The forecast is built from four numbers you type in — monthly income, monthly expenses, current debts, and current savings. That refusal to ask for credentials is deliberate; it is the position the product was designed around.
How accurate is the forecast?
The forecast is a deterministic projection, not a prediction. It compounds the numbers you enter against published assumptions — income growth, expense inflation, retirement returns, debt APRs — over a 120-month horizon. Change an input and the output redraws. Every assumption is documented at /about#methodology, and every revision is dated in the methodology changelog.
What countries do you support for take-home pay?
United States (federal and state) and the United Kingdom (England & Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland) at present. Additional countries are on the roadmap and will be added as we publish their tax tables under the same methodology discipline as the existing ones.

Section 02

Forecast methodology.

Is the tax calculation tax advice?
No. The take-home calculation is an estimate built from published tax brackets and standard deductions. It is not personalised, not certified, and not a substitute for a qualified accountant. We publish the tables we use and the year they apply to so the figure can be checked.
Why don’t you support bank aggregation?
Two reasons, both deliberate. First, asking a visitor for the credentials to their financial institution is a serious thing to ask, and a forecasting tool does not strictly need them — four typed numbers are enough to project the next ten years. Second, the integration layer (Plaid, Yodlee, MX) creates a vendor dependency we are unwilling to take on. We’d rather ship a smaller, sharper tool than a larger, more entangled one.
How accurate is the forecast?
See Getting started above. In short: the forecast is deterministic, the assumptions are published, and the changelog records every revision with a date.

Section 03

Account & billing.

What does Pro include?
Pro adds the scenarios view (carry two forecasts side-by-side), the salary calculator with multi-country tax tables, the report card, and Sage — the AI planner that answers questions in plain English from your numbers. Pro is $9/month; everything in Free remains in Pro.
What’s the Founders Lifetime plan?
A one-time payment of $149 that grants permanent access to every Pro feature, including future modules, with no recurring charge. The plan is capped at 500 seats; once those are filled it closes.
How do I cancel Pro?
There is a single link in settings. Click it once; the cancellation is confirmed immediately, with no retention flow, no “are you sure,” and no requirement to write us. You retain access until the end of the period you paid for. No proration is taken back.
Do you offer refunds?
Yes. Within 14 days of any charge, write to us and we will refund it in full, no questions asked. Outside the 14 days we will still listen, but the policy is the 14-day window.

Section 04

Privacy & data.

Where is my data stored?
In your browser. The numbers you enter are held in local storage on the device you are using and are not transmitted to our servers. If you clear your browser data, the forecast is cleared with it. If you sign in to a paid account, only the metadata required to operate the subscription (email, plan, payment token) leaves the browser; the financial numbers do not.
Can I export my data?
Yes, at any time, in either CSV or JSON. Export is a single click in settings and is not gated by tier. The export contains the full 120-month forecast, the inputs that produced it, and the assumptions in force at the moment of export.
How do I contact support?
By email. The address and a short form live at /contact. We read every message and reply in plain prose, usually within one working day. There is no chatbot in front of the inbox.
