Who we are.

ProFinanceCast is a boutique forecasting house. Based in Europe. Independent. The service is operated by the entity that owns the brand under that name; full company details are available on request.

Throughout these terms, “we” and “ProFinanceCast” refer to that entity. “You” refers to the person using the service, on a free plan or a paid subscription.

What this service does.

ProFinanceCast is a personal-finance forecasting tool. You type a small number of summary figures, the tool projects a ten-year forecast, and you can model scenarios against it. That is the entire product surface. It helps to be clear about what the service is not.

  • Not a bank. We hold no deposits, issue no cards, and process no payments on your behalf.
  • Not a broker. We do not buy, sell, or hold securities for you.
  • Not an investment adviser. We do not give personalised investment advice or manage assets.
  • Not an aggregator. We do not connect to your bank, read your transactions, or hold your credentials.

What you can do with it.

You may use the service for your own personal-finance forecasting and planning, and for the household you support. You may export your numbers at any time in a machine-readable format, cancel at any time, and share screenshots and summaries publicly; the outputs are yours.

Use inside a small self-employed practice or single-person consultancy for your own cash-flow planning is fine. For larger team or organisation use, contact us so we can scope a workspace plan that fits.

What you can’t do.

A short list of things that will get an account suspended.

  • Don’t resell the service. No white-labelling, repackaging, or exposing the outputs through a paid API of your own without a written agreement.
  • Don’t scrape it. Automated extraction beyond ordinary single-user browsing is not permitted. We rate-limit and we notice.
  • Don’t reverse-engineer the forecast. The mathematics is documented in plain prose; do not copy it into a competing product.
  • Don’t abuse the AI advisor. No malicious, defamatory, harassing, or illegal content; no impersonating a regulated adviser; no attempts to extract another party’s private data.
