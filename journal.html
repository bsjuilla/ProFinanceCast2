  1. May 2026 · 11

    Net worth this day $87,420

    The forecasting house, finally on paper.

    Spent the week pricing what a real plan would have cost back when none of this was written down. The answer keeps coming back to the same figure: more than the credit card balance, less than the student loan. The forecast curve sits where it’s supposed to sit. The work now is staying out of its way.

    Filed the next quarterly review for August. Bumped the retirement contribution by 1%. Held everything else.

  2. March 2026 · 28

    Net worth this day $83,710

    Tax season closed without a surprise.

    Refund came in at $1,840. Half went to the credit card; the rest is sitting in the buffer until April rent clears. The credit card is now under $3,500, which is the smallest balance it has been since I started tracking. Avalanche path is on schedule.

    Reading The Psychology of Money for the second time. The chapter on tails is the one that keeps me out of trouble.

  3. December 2025 · 31

    Net worth this day $78,930

    Year-end. Steady, mostly.

    Net worth ended $11,180 above the prior year. Most of that came from the retirement match and the freelance work I picked up in the autumn. December cost more than I planned — $320 over the usual run-rate — but I knew it would and the buffer absorbed it without flinching.

    One resolution: stop tracking the daily figure. Once a month is enough. The volatility on a 30-day view is mostly noise.

  4. August 2025 · 14

    Net worth this day $71,260

    Sold the old car. Added $4,200 to liquid.

    Listed it on a Wednesday, sold it the following Sunday. The buyer didn’t haggle, which I think means I priced it low — but I was happy to be done. The $4,200 went straight to the emergency fund and pushed the buffer past four months of expenses for the first time.

    The household runs fine on one car. We always thought it would; this is the proof.

  5. November 2024 · 02

    Net worth this day $63,440

    The raise landed. Already spoken for.

    The annual review went well. New gross is $95,000; the increase is about $420/mo after tax. Half is going to retirement (auto-bumped the contribution percent the same day), the other half is going to the credit card until it’s gone. I am writing this down so I don’t quietly absorb it into the run-rate over the next three months.

    Lifestyle creep is a real thing. Pre-committing the money before it lands is the only trick I know that works.

  6. July 2025 · 09

    Net worth this day $67,050

    Started the journal.

    First entry. The thinking is simple: I keep making the same money decisions in the same months of the year, and I never remember why I made them. A paragraph a month, paired with the figure, should be enough to fix that.

    Beginning balances, for the record: credit card $4,910 at 24.9%, student loan $6,180 at 6.1%, liquid $18,400, retirement $54,830.
