How this works

Two rules, plain language.

Methodology →
  1. Avalanche pays the minimum on every debt, then steers the entire extra payment to the highest-APR balance. When that balance clears, the freed cash waterfalls to the next-highest APR. Minimises total interest.
  2. Snowball pays the minimum on every debt, then steers the extra payment to the smallest balance. When that balance clears, the freed cash rolls to the next-smallest. Maximises the number of debts crossed off.

For this household, the credit card is both the highest-APR balance and the smallest balance, so the two strategies often converge on the same priority order. The locked summary above reflects the household’s configured scenario; the live strip recomputes whenever you edit an input.
