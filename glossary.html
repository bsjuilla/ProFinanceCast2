Glossary

Six words, defined.

The locked vocabulary the brand uses, in plain prose. Other words on the site read the definitions below.

Definitions

Definitions

Forecast
A deterministic projection of the next ten years, computed month by month from four inputs and a set of published assumptions. It is not a prediction; change an input and the entire 120-month series redraws.
Net worth
The sum of what you own minus the sum of what you owe, at a single point in time. We track it as the anchor metric of the forecast because it summarises both the asset and the debt sides of the household ledger in one number.
Avalanche
A debt-payoff strategy that pays the minimum on every debt and directs any extra payment to the debt with the highest interest rate. Mathematically it minimises total interest paid and is usually the fastest route to zero balance.
Snowball
A debt-payoff strategy that pays the minimum on every debt and directs any extra payment to the debt with the smallest balance. It produces more frequent payoff moments, which some people find easier to sustain even when avalanche would save more interest.
Health score
A single 0–100 number that summarises the household’s position across savings buffer, debt load, and forecast trajectory. The score is a heuristic for orientation, not a credit rating; the methodology is published and the score moves with the underlying inputs.
Take-home pay
Gross salary minus the country-specific income tax, social-insurance, and pension contributions that apply to it. It is the figure that actually lands in your account each month and is the input the forecast uses for income, not the gross headline.
