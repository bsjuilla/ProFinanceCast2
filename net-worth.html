What this assumes

Five inputs, plain language.

Methodology →

The forecast above is a deterministic projection, not a guarantee. Five assumptions shape the curve.

  1. Income growth. Gross income rises 3.0% per year, slightly above long-run wage inflation in the household's market.
  2. Retirement return. Tax-advantaged accounts compound at 7.0% nominal, a conservative read on a sixty-forty allocation after fees.
  3. Inflation. Personal expenses rise 2.5% per year, ahead of headline CPI to account for housing and healthcare drift.
  4. Debt payoff. Existing balances are retired on the avalanche schedule; freed cash redirects to retirement on month 15.
  5. Savings rate. The household saves and invests 30% of monthly surplus through the projection window.

Change any assumption on the settings page and every figure recomputes. The full methodology, including how compounding is applied month-by-month, is documented on the about page.
