Avalanche.

Pay every minimum, every month. Send any extra dollars to the debt with the highest APR first — that is the one accumulating interest fastest, so killing it saves the most. When it clears, its freed-up minimum rolls onto the next-highest-APR debt. Mathematically optimal; emotionally slower if the highest-rate debt is also the largest.

Snowball.

Pay every minimum, every month. Send any extra to the debt with the smallest balance first, regardless of APR. You clear an entire debt sooner, which reads as progress, and the freed-up minimum rolls onto the next-smallest. Costs slightly more in interest most of the time; wins more often on adherence.
